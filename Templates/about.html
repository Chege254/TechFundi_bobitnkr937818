Contact Tracing Application

What Is Contact Tracing?

Contact tracing is the process of identifying, assessing, and managing people who have been exposed to a disease to prevent onward transmission. When systematically applied, contact tracing will break the chains of transmission of infectious virus and is an essential public health tool for controlling the virus. Contact tracing requires identifying people who may have been exposed to virus and following them up daily for 14-28 days from the last point of exposure.

Why Is Contact Tracing Important?

  • To ensure there is limited spread of an infection
  • To find and isolate cases before they erupt
  • To help in identifying the contacts of the patient tested positive for an infection
  • To prevent individual cases from being clusters which in the long run turn into community transmission
